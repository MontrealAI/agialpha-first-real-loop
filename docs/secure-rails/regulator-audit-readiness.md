# Regulator Audit Readiness

## What an auditor may ask

1. What is the intended purpose?
2. Show a real deployment.
3. Show a customer attestation.
4. Show a change that was re-screened.
5. Show an incident or safety ledger.
6. Show that human review is mandatory.
7. Show that auto-merge is disabled.
8. Show that no employment or worker-management use is allowed.
9. Show that no critical-infrastructure safety-component reliance is allowed by default.
10. Show that SecureRails is not a GPAI model provider by default.
11. Show that marketing does not claim cybersecurity certification or guaranteed security.
12. Show that `$AGIALPHA` is utility-only.

## Audit packet

For each deployment, retain:

- Customer Risk Intake.
- Customer Use Attestation.
- Annex III Triage Record.
- Foreseeable Misuse Register.
- Material Modification Log.
- Safety Ledger.
- Claim Boundary Check logs.
- No-Automerge Check logs.
- Human Review records.
- PR Review records.
- Evidence Dockets.
- ProofBundles.
- Incident reports if any.

## Audit answer template

> SecureRails is deployed as an advisory, repo-owned, defensive AI-agent governance and proof-bound remediation system. It produces evidence objects, redacted safety ledgers, replay instructions, and safe PR proposals for human review. It does not decide outcomes about natural persons, does not profile workers, does not function as a critical-infrastructure safety component, does not provide a GPAI model by default, does not perform offensive cyber activity, and does not certify security.

## Regulator response principles

- Answer from evidence, not rhetoric.
- Show deployment records.
- Show excluded-use controls.
- Show human-review logs.
- Show material-change screening.
- Do not rely on enforcement-resource assumptions.
- Do not overstate classification certainty.
- Escalate ambiguous cases to counsel.

## Claim footer

No Evidence Docket, no empirical SOTA claim. Autonomous evidence production is allowed; autonomous claim promotion is not.
